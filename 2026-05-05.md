## Reflections

*On anxiety*
It's okay to plan for predictable things in the short-medium term. It's okay to set goals for the long term. This might be spurred by anxiety, this is its role. You sit down and you deliberately think about something, as much as you can, recognize limitations, make a plan. Great.

It's not useful or responsible to worry constantly, and especially about unpredictable things like war, global economics, the labor market and AI etc. Why? Because
- Nobody actually knows or can know, this is all up in the air.
- It's fake. 

Why fake? Because it appears like it is about the specific thing outside of you. Like, say, career choice. I'm worried because of the specific threat of AI in game development or tech in general. But, the truth of the matter is, no matter what choice I make, I'd be anxious.
- If I chose my  current job, I'd worry about AI replacing all tech everywhere
- If I chose european funding projects, I'd worry about AI taking that job, too, about Romania leaving the EU
- If I chose law, I'd worry about AI taking that job, and also about running into dangerous people who could hurt me
- I don't want to be a tradesman, other jobs that I could go for university for would have similar issues, such as:
	- It takes 6 years to be an architect, this will take too long and I'll be too old and I won't catch up to my previous earnings and AI might replace me too and it's an oversaturated field and I'll be overworked and won't have time for anything else
	- There's no money to be made in quantum mechanics and all the jobs with money are really scarce and I'll have to move to a different country and it takes so many years to get a PhD and I'm not a young upstart in this field 
	- Etc.

It's also obviously a trait of my mind, looking at all other cases of indecision (which are smaller or larger cases of anxiety):
- I worry about trivial things which steal away the fun out of my favorite hobby (video games), such as: worrying that I won't have maximum fun on a certain class in an MMORPG, or about the MMORPG itself
- I worry that I'll lose my hair because I'll get in shape and get a girlfriend
- I worry that I'll never get to work in Japan because I'll be too old by the time that becomes a possibility
- I worry that the company I work for will go under and I won't be able to financially recover and we'll default on our debts and be kicked out of our apartment
- I worry that I won't get good at gamedev in time for this  to be a viable career for me
- I worry that I'll get old and sick soon
- I worry about my mom's health and my brother's future
- I worry about losing contact with my cousin after he starts working as a dentist
- I worry about having to leave my mom, my brother and my cats behind because I'll want to move in with my eventual future girlfriend or for a job that won't accept remote work
- I used to worry about never having the determination to lose weight and get in shape, but I have been steadily losing weight and getting in shape for the last couple months so that's gotten better.

Here are a couple of rational responses/realizations/insights/etc.:
- A genuine attempt at gamedev for a year won't tank my future chances, given that I'm still working 2 jobs in different fields (AI software dev + european funding projects).
- Not trying gamedev won't make me not anxious anymore (as outlined above, I'd worry about any career choice).
- Not trying gamedev would introduce *horrible regret/guilt* into my life.
- Anxiety seems to be an emotional tendency, far exceeding any useful reminders for caution.
- Anxiety steals the joy out of any activity or future prospect, anything exciting is followed by "but what if this fails catastrophically and my life is over?"
- The coping mechanism for anxiety that I seem to have developed is just trying to think very hard (like trying to simulate the world economy in my head or geopolitics etc.) and searching online for opinions which reassure me (like "the AI bubble will burst" etc.), which
	- Leaves me very drained and further steals joy from any activity
	- Takes up literal hours of my day as I sit in bed worrying
	- Makes me sad and frustrated
	- Makes my mood dependent on what posts I've seen that day

So, I think I should try this:
- Dedicated sessions of structured time where I intentionally worry about the future and make decisions if need be, maybe once a week.
- Recognize anxiety as a feeling or a thought and not reality (I think they call this "diffusion").
- Accept that, at least right now, there is tendency inside of me. 
- Remember to make decisions with the part of my mind that is not anxious, I listen to the anxiety but I am not the same as it.
- Most of the time, acknowledge that anxiety is there and say "I appreciate that you want to help me, we will discuss whatever you wish during our dedicated session" instead of listening to what it wants to tell me right then, and then gently return to the task I was working on.
- A lot of anxiety comes from another tendency of mine, oft-explored lately, of perfectionism, because I worry I cannot be perfect in some way (perfectly having fun with games, perfectly good-looking with hair, for instance). Anxiety about aging comes from something that I can't fix (namely... aging), and so can NEVER make perfect after some culturally-assigned point of youth. Letting go of this cold grip on perfection will simultaneously allow me to feel less anxious. Asking why it has to be perfect and finding no real answer helps.

*On joy*
Lately I've been frustrated that I don't wake up full of excitement and joy and curiosity for the day ahead. I think that:
- Anxiety steals a lot of that away, especially if (as I do) I open up BBC first thing in the morning to check on world news, hoping for some good reassuring updates on the state of the world. Most of the time, I get mixed, vague signals that don't put my mind at ease.
- Joy and probably all moods come from doing things, not before them. Doing things that matter to me (like gamedev) *is joy* and it makes me *feel joyful* after (even though my mind does instantly protest by crying that it's useless, it's not enough, it's too late, I should have started years ago, what if AI steals our job etc.)
	- I think, eventually, with enough deliberate practice on noticing our mind's maladapted, wounded habits, this will get better, it should not discourage me.
- Allowing myself to do the things I care about and cultivating a healthy, accepting, open and reasonably tranquil attitude makes space for more joy, and it reshapes the mind. I've noticed (as psychologists have noticed clinically) that the mind produces thoughts and subsequent feelings based on what our attention is taken by. A simple example is that if I browse the league of legends subreddit, I'm more likely to install and play league. If I watch a lot of gameplay for a video game, I start thinking about and feeling the want for playing that video game. This is why ads work also, I think. Anyway, in the context of my personal life and well-being, participating in things I find meaningful and love (gamedev, art,  DnD, board games, video games, magic the gathering, socialization, friendship, romance, community project, teaching people things, skills etc.) will make it so my mind starts to produce, on its own, positive thoughts and emotions related to those things. In the end, that will align my mind with my values, with what is good and just in this world, and that is what joy and excitement and curiosity are, and where they spring from.
- I already feel lighter, happier, more at ease, just from freeing myself from negative patterns of thought and feeling, from being compassionate and kind to myself, from freeing myself from the burdens of unnecessary suffering and anxiety. So the capacity is definitely still in there, and it's not far away, ever.
