## Vegan Schnitzel
### 1. The Best Option: Homemade Seitan (Wheat Gluten)

If you want a true, meaty texture with actual structure and a satisfying bite, seitan is king. It doesn't taste like bread; it mimics the dense protein fibers of meat.

- **How it works:** You buy **Vital Wheat Gluten** powder (often found in health food stores, bio shops, or online easily). You mix it with water/broth and spices to form a dough, knead it to develop the gluten strings, and then simmer or steam it.
    
- **The Schnitzel Technique:** To get a tender, non-rubbery schnitzel, mix a bit of mashed chickpeas or tofu into your gluten dough (this breaks up the gluten bonds so it isn't _too_ chewy). Shape it into flat cutlets, simmer it in a rich vegetable broth for 45 minutes, let it cool, and then bread and fry it.
    

## 2. The Whole-Food Champions: Mushrooms

Since you haven't tried mushrooms yet, you are missing out! They naturally have an incredible texture that doesn't feel spongy if cooked correctly.

- **Pleurotus (Oyster Mushrooms):** These are widely available in Romanian supermarkets and are arguably the _best_ natural chicken/pork substitute. They grow in clusters of flat, fleshy caps.
    
- **The Schnitzel Technique:** Take a large, flat oyster mushroom cap. Do **not** boil it. Instead, press it down in a hot, dry pan with another heavy pan on top of it for a few minutes. This squeezes out the excess water and compresses the fibers, making it dense and meaty. After pressing, dip it in your batter, coat with breadcrumbs, and fry. It is a total game-changer.
    

## 3. The Tofu Fix (Without Freezing It)

Don't give up on that **Inedit** tofu just yet, but change the technique entirely. Stop freezing it! Freezing destroys its structural density.

- **The Schnitzel Technique (The Slab Method):** Take the fresh tofu straight from the package, wrap it in a clean towel, and put a heavy book on it for 20 minutes just to get the surface moisture off. Cut it into thick slices. Instead of a wet marinade (which makes it soggy), press a dry spice rub directly into the tofu blocks.
    
- **The Batter Trick:** Dip the dry, seasoned tofu slabs into a thick batter made of flour, water/plant milk, and a spoonful of mustard (this acts as the "egg"), then press it firmly into standard breadcrumbs (**Pesmet**). Fry it in plenty of hot oil. Because the inside wasn't frozen, it stays dense, smooth, and firm rather than spongy.
    

## The Ultimate Vegan "Egg" Wash & Breading

For any of these options, the batter is crucial for getting that shatteringly crisp crust. Skip the thin flax-egg or plain plant milk. Use this three-step station:

1. **Dredge:** Lightly coat your mushroom, seitan, or tofu in plain flour (seasoned with salt, garlic powder, and onion powder).
    
2. **The "Egg" Dip:** Create a thick liquid (the consistency of heavy cream) using **water, a few tablespoons of flour, and a teaspoon of mustard**. The mustard adds fats and emulsifiers that mimic egg yolk, helping the breadcrumbs stick and get crispy.
    
3. **The Crunch:** Press it firmly into fine breadcrumbs (**pesmet**). For an extra-crispy crunch, you can mix in a few crushed cornflakes.
    

If you want something that truly mimics a pork or chicken schnitzel, I highly recommend picking up a pack of oyster mushrooms (_ciuperci pleurotus_) on your next grocery run and trying the pressing method.
